Dorothy Vaughan

First African American manager at NASA

Here is a time line of Dorothy Vaughan's life:

  • 1910 - Born in Kansas City, Missouri
  • 1917 - Moves to Morgantown, West Virginia
  • 1925 - Graduates from Beechurst High School as valedictorian
  • 1929 - Graduates with a B.A. in mathematics from Wilberforce University in Wilberforce, Ohio
  • 1932 - Marries Howard Vaughan and moves to Newport News, Virginia where they have six children together
  • 1943 - Hired as a mathematician at the Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia where she performed calculations for flight paths, computer programming, and the Scout Project
  • 1949 - Promoted to acting head of the West Area Computers, becoming the first African American supervisor at the NACA and one of few female supervisors
  • 1961 - Taught herself and her coworkers FORTRAN and moved into the electronic computing department when digital computers were brought to the NACA.
  • 1971 - Retired from NASA after 28 years
  • November 20, 2008 - Died at age 98 in Hampton, Virginia
